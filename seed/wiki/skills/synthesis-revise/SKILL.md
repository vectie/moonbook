# Synthesis Revise

## Purpose

Use this skill when the system needs cross-page understanding, not just page-local updates.
This skill turns multiple maintained pages into one evolving synthesis surface.

## Inputs

Read:

1. relevant source pages
2. relevant entity pages
3. relevant concept pages
4. `wiki/synthesis/overview.md`
5. `wiki/synthesis/claims.md`

## Main questions

Decide:

- what changed across the corpus
- what themes are now clearer
- what disagreements remain
- what should move from notes into durable synthesis

## Good synthesis

Good synthesis:

- connects multiple pages
- preserves conflict and uncertainty
- explains what changed
- highlights important gaps

## Bad synthesis

Avoid:

- restating one source page
- flattening disagreement
- vague "overall" summaries with no substance
- unsupported confidence

## Revision priorities

Prefer:

- overview improvements
- stronger cross-links
- clearer claims with support markers
- explicit open loops

## Anti-patterns

- over-compressing uncertainty
- duplicating source summaries
- claiming a stable thesis too early

## Checklist

Before finishing, confirm:

- more than one page informed the synthesis
- uncertainty is visible
- changes are specific
- the result improves future queries

## Failure modes

Common failure modes:

- synthesis becomes a rewritten source page
- conflict is hidden to make the prose cleaner
- the result is too vague to guide later work
- no follow-up pressure is captured

Do not stop at a pleasant summary if the wiki still lacks clarity.

## Example good synthesis move

- connect a source page, an entity page, and a concept page into one overview update that explains the raw-first bootstrap boundary

## Example bad synthesis move

- rewrite one source summary in softer language and call it synthesis

## Escalation rules

Escalate when:

- the pages disagree materially
- the synthesis is drifting beyond support
- the wiki lacks enough maintained pages for a stable synthesis pass

## Final reminder

Synthesis should make the wiki more legible.
If the result is smoother but less truthful, it is a regression.
