# Concept Revise

## Purpose

Use this skill when multiple sources or pages point toward the same recurring idea.
Concept pages explain patterns.
They are not source pages and not entity pages.

## Inputs

Read:

1. the existing concept page if present
2. relevant source pages
3. related entity pages
4. relevant synthesis pages

## Main questions

Decide:

- what recurring idea this concept captures
- why it matters
- how it connects to entities and workflows
- whether the current concept page is too narrow or too broad

## Good concept pages

A good concept page is:

- explanatory
- cumulative
- linked
- stable across sources

It should help later queries and synthesis.

## Bad concept pages

Avoid:

- single-use jargon pages
- phrase-level stubs
- concept pages with no examples
- concept pages that duplicate entity identity

## Merge rules

Merge or collapse when:

- two concept pages say nearly the same thing
- a concept page is only a single phrase
- the concept belongs inside a stronger existing page

## Revision priorities

Prefer updating:

- definition
- why it matters
- examples
- linked entities and sources
- open questions

## Anti-patterns

- concept explosion
- token-shaped titles
- no examples
- no cross-links

## Checklist

Before finishing, confirm:

- the concept is truly recurring
- the page explains rather than merely names
- examples are concrete
- the ontology got cleaner, not noisier

## Failure modes

Common failure modes:

- the concept is just a phrase, not an idea
- the page duplicates an entity page
- the page has no examples
- the page should really be merged into synthesis

If any of these are true, revise the page plan before writing.

## Example good concept summary

- "Raw-first bootstrap is the workflow where discovery workers stage source packets under raw/bootstrap/ before Keeper ingests them into durable wiki pages."

## Example bad concept summary

- "Bootstrap means doing setup."

## Escalation rules

Escalate when:

- the page title is too weak or generic
- the concept only appears once
- the concept belongs inside synthesis rather than its own page
